A point charge creates an electric field that points directly away from or toward itself. A formula to compute the magnitude of the field was provided in the last section. Here we will consider how to determine the (Cartesian) components of the field vector.

The trick is that the displacement vector, starting at the charge and ending at the "field point" has the same direction as the field vector. Therefore, the right triangles formed by the components of both vectors are similar.
